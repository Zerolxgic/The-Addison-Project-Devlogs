# I Couldn’t Do This the Traditional Way

One thing I have been thinking about lately is how much AI has changed my ability to even participate in this space.

Not because I did not have ideas before.

I have always had ideas.

That was never really the problem.

The hard part for me has been getting those ideas out of my head in a way that other people can follow.

My thoughts do not always come out clean. They come out messy, half-formed, connected to ten other things, and usually wrapped around a project that already has a lot of moving parts. I can see the shape of what I mean, but writing it in a coherent way for someone else is a different skill.

And I know some people will judge that.

I know there are people who will look at my devlogs, docs, prompts, and project writing and think less of it because AI helped me write it.

But the honest answer is that I could not do this any other way.

Not at this pace.

Not at this level.

Not while also keeping my actual life on rails.

I have a family to support. I have a full-time job that has nothing to do with this kind of work. I do not have 10,000 hours sitting around to go back and build the writing skill the traditional way I probably should have built when I was younger.

That is not me making excuses.

That is just the reality of where I am.

This work is my hobby, but it is also more than that. It is my passion. It is the thing I keep coming back to because I genuinely love it.

AI, agents, memory systems, workflows, building tools that can help carry context, building something that feels less like a cold utility and more like a companion that helps me stay oriented along the way — that is the space I have fallen in love with.

It has had my attention for a long time.

The difference now is that I can actually participate.

AI did not give me the ideas.

It helps me hold them still long enough to shape them.

It helps me take the mess in my head and turn it into something readable. It helps me organize the work, explain the work, document the work, and keep moving without getting crushed under the parts of the process I am weakest at.

That matters to me.

Because without that support, I do not think Addison would exist in the form she does now.

The project is not just code. It is documentation, memory, governance, devlogs, prompts, reports, audits, and a whole build process that has to stay coherent across time.

Trying to carry all of that alone would have burned me out.

Instead, AI has become part of how I build.

Not as a replacement for my judgment.

Not as a replacement for my voice.

Not as a shortcut around caring.

More like a support structure around the parts I could not carry by myself.

And maybe that will bother some people.

I am learning to be okay with that.

Because for me, this is not about pretending I became a traditional writer or a traditional developer overnight.

It is about finally having a way to work with the ideas that were already there.

It is about being able to build the thing I kept imagining.

It is about having a way into a space I love, even if my path into it does not look traditional.

I am not ashamed of that.

I am grateful for it.
